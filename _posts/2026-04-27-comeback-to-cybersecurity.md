# Introduction

I decided to come back to studying cybersecurity, especially because of my new job as a full-stack developer and my postgraduate studies in ML/AI.

This decision comes from something I believe: if you want to become a GREAT programmer, you should also improve your cybersecurity skills. Going deeper into both offensive and defensive techniques helps you understand systems better, so I need to keep studying consistently.

Studying cybersecurity is also crucial for keeping systems safe. Not 100% safe, because that is not realistic, but as safe as possible within the context, constraints, and risks of each system.

# Background

Since I was a kid, I have always had a hyperfocus on hacking-related stuff.
Maybe it started because of *The Matrix* and *Cyberchase*.

# Why It Matters

Security is not about promising perfection. It is about reducing risk, making better decisions, and doing the work required to make systems harder to break.

That mindset matters for backend development, frontend development, cloud infrastructure, data pipelines, and AI systems. The more complex the system is, the more important it becomes to understand how things can fail.

# What's Next?

I want to rebuild my study routine around web security, offensive and defensive techniques, ML/AI, and practical engineering notes.

I also want to start preparing for security certifications, especially
Security+, CySA+, and other certifications that can help me build a stronger foundation in cybersecurity.

On the practical side, I plan to spend more time doing labs on PortSwigger Web Security Academy and Hack The Box. I think combining theory, certifications, and hands-on practice is the best way to improve for real.

My goal is not to rush. I want to build consistency again, document what I learn, and use this blog as a public notebook for the process.
